# Professional Self-Assessment

Completing the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University has been a defining experience in my development as a software professional. Throughout the program, I have strengthened my technical abilities, expanded my understanding of core computer science principles, and learned how to apply these skills to real-world problems. Building my ePortfolio for CS 499 allowed me to revisit earlier work, enhance it with new knowledge, and demonstrate my growth across software engineering, algorithms and data structures, and database management. This process helped me clearly see how far I have come and how prepared I am to enter the field with confidence.

One of the most valuable aspects of the program has been learning how to collaborate effectively in team environments. Courses such as CS 250 and CS 320 emphasized communication, version control, and shared problem-solving. These experiences taught me how to contribute to group discussions, document my work clearly, and support team decision-making. I also learned how to communicate with stakeholders in a way that is both professional and accessible, ensuring that technical decisions are understood by both technical and non-technical audiences.

Throughout the program, I gained a strong foundation in data structures and algorithms, especially through CS 300. I learned how to evaluate the efficiency of different approaches, choose appropriate data structures, and design solutions that balance performance and maintainability. These skills became especially important when enhancing my algorithms artifact, where I improved the logic and structure of my original work to create a more efficient solution.

My coursework also strengthened my abilities in software engineering and database development. In IT 145, I learned the fundamentals of application development and object-oriented programming, which I later expanded on during my software engineering enhancement. In DAD 220, I developed a solid understanding of relational databases, SQL, and data modeling. Enhancing this artifact allowed me to rebuild a complete database environment, refine the schema, and incorporate more realistic data—all of which reflect practical, industry-relevant skills.

Security has been another important theme throughout the program. I learned how to identify vulnerabilities, validate data, and design systems with a security-first mindset. Even in projects that were not explicitly security-focused, I learned to consider how data is handled, how user input is processed, and how design decisions can impact long-term safety and reliability.

Together, the artifacts in my ePortfolio demonstrate the full range of my computer science abilities. My IT 145 enhancement highlights my growth in software engineering and design. My CS 300 enhancement showcases my understanding of algorithms, data structures, and problem-solving. My DAD 220 enhancement demonstrates my ability to design, build, and refine relational databases using SQL and industry-standard tools. Each artifact represents a different stage of my development, and together they form a cohesive picture of my skills, strengths, and readiness for professional work.

Building this ePortfolio has helped me reflect on my progress, refine my technical abilities, and prepare for the next steps in my career. I am proud of the work I have completed throughout this program, and I am confident that the skills I have gained—combined with the artifacts showcased here—will support my goals as I move forward into the field of computer science.
