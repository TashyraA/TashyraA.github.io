# Professional Self-Assessment

Completing the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University has been a defining experience in my development as a software professional. Throughout the program, I have strengthened my technical abilities, expanded my understanding of core computer science principles, and learned how to apply these skills to real-world problems. Building my ePortfolio for CS 499 allowed me to revisit earlier work, enhance it with new knowledge, and demonstrate my growth across software engineering, algorithms and data structures, and database management.

One of the most valuable aspects of the program has been learning how to collaborate effectively in team environments. Courses such as CS 250 and CS 320 emphasized communication, version control, and shared problem-solving. These experiences taught me how to contribute to group discussions, document my work clearly, and support team decision-making. I also learned how to communicate with stakeholders in a way that is both professional and accessible.

Throughout the program, I gained a strong foundation in data structures and algorithms, especially through CS 300. I learned how to evaluate the efficiency of different approaches, choose appropriate data structures, and design solutions that balance performance and maintainability. These skills became especially important when enhancing my algorithms artifact.

My coursework also strengthened my abilities in software engineering and database development. In IT 145, I learned the fundamentals of application development and object-oriented programming. In DAD 220, I developed a solid understanding of relational databases, SQL, and data modeling. Enhancing these artifacts allowed me to refine my technical abilities and apply more advanced concepts.

Security has been another important theme throughout the program. I learned how to identify vulnerabilities, validate data, and design systems with a security-first mindset.

Together, the artifacts in my ePortfolio demonstrate the full range of my computer science abilities. Each artifact represents a different stage of my development, and together they form a cohesive picture of my skills, strengths, and readiness for professional work.

I am proud of the work I have completed throughout this program, and I am confident that the skills I have gained—combined with the artifacts showcased here—will support my goals as I move forward into the field of computer science.
